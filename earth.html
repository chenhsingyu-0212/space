Introduction

Earth is the third planet among the eight planets in the solar system, possessing one satellite—the Moon (commonly known as Luna), situated between Venus and Mars. The average distance from Earth to the Sun is one astronomical unit, approximately 150 million kilometers.

The Physical Appearance of Earth

Earth is the only planet in the solar system with significant amounts of liquid water on its surface, with about 70% covered by oceans, which are crucial for nurturing life. When viewed from space, Earth appears as a beautiful blue planet enveloped in a layer of white clouds rich in water vapor. Although water has been found elsewhere in the solar system, most of it exists in ice form.

The Surface Environment of Earth

Earth is currently the only known planet in the solar system that supports life, with surface temperatures ranging approximately from -60°C to 50°C, which allows for conditions that are neither too hot nor too cold. Because Earth's axis of rotation is tilted at about 23.44 degrees, it experiences seasonal changes.
